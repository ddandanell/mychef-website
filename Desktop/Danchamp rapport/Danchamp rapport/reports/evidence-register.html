Contradictions and tension points to resolve

Cross-source cleanup required
Issue Conflicting inputs Working handling rule
Free-to-paid conversion Pasted notes mention ~2% annual conversion; vision and other materials imply 5-8% or 6% assumptions. Show as unresolved scenario range until validation data exists.
Private user acquisition target Pasted notes mention minimum 100 new users monthly and also 1000+ ongoing; vision deck shows 4,000+ in first phase. Use conservative monthly acquisition in financial scenarios; keep larger growth as upside only.
DanCham pilot vs regional platform thesis Board and operational documents focus on low-risk validation and MVP budgets; Nordic market report adds a 46-organization, regional scale case plus IDR 14B Stage 1 investment and IDR 56B pre-money framing. Never present the regional investor thesis as if it were the same decision as DanCham Phase 0 validation.
Budget totals Instructions cite IDR 120-200M for Phase 0-2, while master reference shows much larger 18-month totals with later phases included. Always state which phases are included and avoid blending them.
Strategic ambition Vision deck stretches toward broader Europe-scale expat platform language; board material is intentionally narrow and cautious. Use ambition language only where the audience benefits from long-range upside framing.

Claim handling rules for the full package

Editorial standard
  1. Keep commitments conditional unless they are explicitly confirmed.
  2. Separate facts, assumptions, scenarios, and recommendations in every report.
  3. Do not use board-safe language and investor-upside language interchangeably.
  4. Do not use regional market size or investor valuation logic to imply that the DanCham board is being asked to approve that scale of ambition now.
  5. If a number is still validation-dependent, label it that way at point of use.
  6. If a narrative is compelling but unsupported, either downgrade it to a hypothesis or remove it.
